Menstrual Phase

Estrogen and progesterone are at their lowest. Your uterine lining sheds and your body enters its most inward, restorative state. This is your winter — a time for deep rest and reflection, not pushing through.

⚡ Energy & Mood

  • Energy is naturally lower — honour it
  • Heightened intuition and introspection
  • Possible cramping, fatigue, or lower back tension
  • Emotional sensitivity — be gentle with yourself

🥗 Nutrition Focus

  • Iron-rich foods: lentils, red meat, leafy greens
  • Anti-inflammatory: ginger, turmeric, omega-3s
  • Warming meals: soups, broths, cooked vegetables
  • Dark chocolate (70%+) for magnesium
  • Reduce salt and alcohol to ease bloating

🧘 Movement

  • Yin yoga and gentle stretching
  • Slow walks in nature
  • Rest is a legitimate training strategy
  • Heat therapy for cramps (hot water bottle, bath)

🌙 Self-Care

  • Prioritise extra sleep — your body is working hard
  • Journaling and reflection
  • Reduce social obligations where possible
  • Warm baths with magnesium salts

💡 Lifestyle Tips

  • Cancel what doesn't serve you — it's valid
  • Limit screens in the evening
  • Review the past month; set intentions
  • Slow, nourishing morning routines

🌸 Hormone Profile

  • Estrogen: lowest of the cycle
  • Progesterone: at baseline
  • FSH begins to rise toward end of phase
  • Cortisol sensitivity is higher — manage stress
🌱
Follicular Phase

Estrogen begins its beautiful rise as follicles develop in preparation for ovulation. Energy, creativity, and optimism return. This is your spring — a time of renewal, fresh starts, and building momentum.

⚡ Energy & Mood

  • Rising energy and enthusiasm
  • Creativity peaks — great for brainstorming
  • Social, optimistic, and mentally sharp
  • Motivation to start new projects returns

🥗 Nutrition Focus

  • Light, fresh, and vibrant foods
  • Fermented foods to support estrogen metabolism
  • Sprouts, leafy greens, and cruciferous vegetables
  • Lean proteins to support muscle building
  • Flaxseeds for phytoestrogen balance

🏋️ Movement

  • Begin increasing workout intensity
  • Strength training responds well now
  • Try a new class or activity
  • Cardio and HIIT feel accessible again

🌿 Self-Care

  • Social connection and collaboration
  • Creative projects and vision boarding
  • Plan your month ahead with this energy
  • Skincare focus — estrogen supports skin radiance

💡 Lifestyle Tips

  • Best phase to start new habits
  • Tackle challenging tasks and decisions
  • Book appointments, meetings, and events
  • Explore and experiment — you're resilient now

🌸 Hormone Profile

  • Estrogen: steadily rising
  • FSH: stimulating follicle development
  • Progesterone: still low
  • Testosterone: beginning gentle rise
🌕
Ovulatory Phase

Your LH surge triggers ovulation and estrogen peaks alongside a brief testosterone spike. This is your summer — peak confidence, vitality, communication, and magnetism. Your body is designed to shine right now.

⚡ Energy & Mood

  • Peak physical and mental energy
  • Confidence, charisma, and warmth
  • Communication and persuasion at their best
  • High motivation and drive

🥗 Nutrition Focus

  • Raw vegetables and high-fibre foods
  • Support estrogen clearance with cruciferous veg
  • Antioxidant-rich: berries, colourful produce
  • Zinc (pumpkin seeds, chickpeas) for ovulation support
  • Light, cooling meals if energy runs high

🏋️ Movement

  • Peak performance window — go for PRs
  • HIIT, heavy lifting, competitive sport
  • High-intensity cardio feels effortless
  • Group classes and team workouts thrive

🌿 Self-Care

  • Lean into your social energy
  • Important conversations and presentations
  • Dates, networking, and public events
  • Capture your ideas — creativity is high

💡 Lifestyle Tips

  • Schedule high-stakes meetings or pitches
  • Lead, speak, and advocate for yourself
  • Your empathy is at its peak — connect deeply
  • Be aware: ovulation window lasts only 12–24 hours

🌸 Hormone Profile

  • Estrogen: at its peak
  • LH: surges to trigger ovulation
  • Testosterone: brief but meaningful spike
  • Progesterone: begins rising post-ovulation
🍂
Luteal Phase

Progesterone rises to prepare the uterus for possible implantation, then falls if pregnancy doesn't occur. This is your autumn — a time for completing, organising, nesting, and gently winding down. PMS, if it occurs, happens in the late luteal phase.

⚡ Energy & Mood

  • Energy gradually slows — honour the shift
  • Detail-oriented and nesting instinct
  • Possible PMS in final days: irritability, bloating
  • Emotional processing and sensitivity increases

🥗 Nutrition Focus

  • Complex carbs: sweet potato, oats, brown rice
  • Magnesium (dark chocolate, leafy greens, nuts)
  • B vitamins to support mood regulation
  • Calcium-rich foods for PMS symptom reduction
  • Reduce caffeine and refined sugar late in phase

🧘 Movement

  • Moderate, enjoyable movement
  • Pilates, swimming, barre, and yoga
  • Long walks over intense cardio
  • Begin transitioning to rest as your period nears

🌿 Self-Care

  • Nesting rituals: tidy, organise, prepare
  • Journaling and emotional check-ins
  • Reduce commitments in the final days
  • Skin may need extra hydration — support the barrier

💡 Lifestyle Tips

  • Finish and wrap up projects — not start them
  • Limit alcohol: progesterone amplifies its effects
  • Herbal teas: chamomile, raspberry leaf, lemon balm
  • Set gentle boundaries and protect your energy

🌸 Hormone Profile

  • Progesterone: peaks mid-luteal, then drops
  • Estrogen: secondary smaller peak, then declines
  • Both drop sharply if no implantation occurs
  • This hormonal drop triggers menstruation
Cycle Nutrition

Food is information. Your hormones are listening.

What you eat directly influences how your hormones function, how you feel, and how your cycle unfolds. Cycle-synced nutrition isn't a trend — it's one of the most powerful things you can do for your health.

🌱

Hormone-Supportive Foods

Prioritise fibre, healthy fats, and quality protein to give your body the building blocks for balanced hormones every phase.

💊

Targeted Supplements

Magnesium glycinate, omega-3s, vitamin D3+K2, and a quality B-complex are evidence-informed foundations for cycle wellness.

💧

Hydration Strategy

Your hydration needs shift through your cycle. Electrolytes during the luteal phase and warm herbal teas during menstruation support your body beautifully.

🚫

What to Limit

Excess caffeine, alcohol, and ultra-processed foods all disrupt hormonal signalling. Small consistent swaps create lasting change.

Pregnancy & Postpartum

Growing a life is the ultimate act of wellness

Whether you're preparing to conceive, navigating each trimester, or recovering postpartum — this space was built for you. Nourishing, evidence-informed, and deeply supportive.

Before Conception

Preparing your body for pregnancy

The 3–6 months before conception are arguably the most important investment you can make in your future pregnancy. Optimising your hormones, nutrient stores, and cycle health now sets the foundation for everything that follows.

🥗 Nutrition Foundations

  • Begin 400mcg+ folate (or methylfolate) immediately
  • Iron, B12, and vitamin D levels checked
  • Omega-3s for egg quality and inflammation
  • Reduce ultra-processed foods and alcohol
  • CoQ10 to support egg and sperm quality

🔬 Hormone Optimisation

  • Track your cycle for 3+ months to understand your baseline
  • Identify ovulation with LH strips or basal body temp
  • Support thyroid and adrenal health
  • Reduce endocrine disruptors in home & beauty products

🧘 Mindset & Lifestyle

  • Begin stress management practices now
  • Reduce high-intensity training to moderate
  • Prioritise 7–9 hours of quality sleep
  • Build your support network early

🏥 Medical Prep

  • Pre-conception GP or midwife appointment
  • Full blood panel: iron, B12, D, thyroid, folate
  • Dental check (oral health affects pregnancy)
  • Review any medications with your doctor
Weeks 1–12

First Trimester — the quiet miracle

Your body is building an entirely new life from scratch, and most of it is invisible. Fatigue and nausea are not weakness — they're signs of profound hormonal work happening beneath the surface. Rest is your highest priority right now.

🥗 Nutrition

  • Small, frequent meals to manage nausea
  • Ginger tea, crackers, cold foods easier to tolerate
  • Continue folate; add choline (eggs, liver)
  • Iron-rich foods — blood volume is expanding
  • Don't stress perfection: any food is better than none

🧘 Safe Movement

  • Gentle walking and light yoga
  • Continue previous exercise if feeling well
  • Avoid overheating (hot yoga, saunas)
  • Rest when your body asks — it always knows

💆 Emotional Wellness

  • Anxiety and mixed emotions are normal and valid
  • Limit who you tell until you're comfortable
  • Journal your experience — it's precious
  • Connect with your partner or support person

📋 What to Expect

  • Fatigue unlike anything you've felt before
  • Breast tenderness and frequent urination
  • Nausea peaks weeks 6–9 for most women
  • First scan typically at 8–12 weeks
Weeks 13–26

Second Trimester — your golden window

For many women, the second trimester brings a beautiful return of energy, a visible bump, and the first movements of your baby. This is a time to nourish deeply, move joyfully, and connect with your changing body.

🥗 Nutrition

  • Increase calories by ~300–350/day now
  • Calcium and vitamin D for bone development
  • Protein at every meal for baby's growth
  • Omega-3 DHA for brain and eye development
  • Constipation common — fibre and hydration key

🏋️ Movement

  • Prenatal yoga and swimming are ideal
  • Strength training with modifications welcome
  • Avoid lying flat on back after 20 weeks
  • Pelvic floor work begins in earnest now

💆 Mind & Body

  • Baby movements typically felt 16–24 weeks
  • Anatomy scan at ~20 weeks — a milestone
  • Begin birth preferences exploration
  • Hypnobirthing, mindfulness, and breathwork

🌿 Self-Care

  • Belly oil for skin elasticity (rosehip, jojoba)
  • Prenatal massage if available to you
  • Begin building your birth team
  • Set up your support structure for postpartum
Weeks 27–40+

Third Trimester — preparing to meet them

Your body is in its final remarkable stretch. Baby is growing rapidly, your energy may slow, and your mind turns naturally toward preparation and nesting. This is a time of deep readiness — physical and emotional.

🥗 Nutrition

  • Small frequent meals as baby crowds stomach
  • Dates from 36 weeks (research supports cervical ripening)
  • Raspberry leaf tea from 36+ weeks
  • Iron levels monitored as labour approaches
  • Stay hydrated — Braxton Hicks worsen with dehydration

🧘 Movement

  • Walking, swimming, and gentle yoga remain safe
  • Pelvic floor and perineal preparation
  • Spinning Babies techniques for optimal positioning
  • Rest when you need it — it's valid always

💆 Birth Preparation

  • Write your birth preferences (not a rigid plan)
  • Practice breathing and relaxation techniques
  • Perineal massage from 34–36 weeks
  • Pack your bag from 36 weeks

🌿 Nesting & Mindset

  • Set up postpartum support in advance
  • Batch-cook and freeze nourishing meals
  • Communicate your needs clearly to your support team
  • Fear is normal — breathe through it with information
The Fourth Trimester

Postpartum — the most important recovery of your life

You just grew and birthed a human being. Postpartum recovery is not about bouncing back. It's about rebuilding — slowly, nourished, and supported. The world rushes this. We don't. Give yourself 12 months minimum.

🥗 Recovery Nutrition

  • Iron-rich foods to restore losses from birth
  • Protein and collagen for tissue healing
  • Continue prenatal vitamins — especially if breastfeeding
  • Extra calories if breastfeeding (+400–500/day)
  • Warm, nourishing, easy-to-digest foods

🏥 Physical Recovery

  • No exercise beyond walking for 6–8 weeks minimum
  • Pelvic floor physiotherapy — essential, not optional
  • Diastasis recti screening before returning to exercise
  • Scar massage for C-section from 6 weeks

💆 Mental Health

  • Baby blues (days 3–10) are normal and hormonally driven
  • Postnatal depression affects 1 in 5 women — seek help
  • Sleep deprivation is profound — ask for help
  • Identity shifts are real and deserve acknowledgement

🤱 Breastfeeding Support

  • It's a learned skill — give yourself grace
  • Lactation consultant is worth every penny
  • Supply is typically established by 6 weeks
  • Fed is always best — formula is never failure
